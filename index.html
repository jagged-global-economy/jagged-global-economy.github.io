We introduce a national AI exposure metric that combines occupation-level exposure scores and international employment data for 141 countries. The metric captures how strongly a national labor market aligns with tasks frontier AI can already accelerate or transform; it is not a forecast of adoption, wages, or net employment losses.

Key Findings

  1. National AI exposure varies substantially across countries. Luxembourg is 2.6× more exposed than Burundi, the least exposed country in the measured sample.
  2. High-income countries are more exposed. Europe & Central Asia are 50% more exposed than Sub-Saharan Africa, reflecting cross-country differences in occupational composition.
  3. Women are more exposed than men in 91% of studied countries. The gender gap is driven by women's concentration in white-collar and sales occupations, with exceptions where women's employment remains concentrated in agriculture and household enterprises.
  4. White-collar work explains much of national exposure. White-collar employment share strongly predicts national AI exposure, showing how labor composition shapes the global exposure map.
  5. Exposure predicts observed AI adoption. National exposure predicts usage statistics published by Anthropic, OpenAI, and Microsoft; a 0.10 increase in exposure corresponds to 12× growth in per-capita Claude usage and 19% higher national generative AI adoption rate in the fitted relationships.
  6. Remittances create indirect exposure. Countries dependent on income earned abroad can inherit exposure from the labor markets where migrant workers earn remittance income.

Darker countries are more exposed to frontier AI. Countries without 2-digit ISCO-08 employment data are shown in gray.

Cross-Country Labor Composition

National exposure differs because countries allocate labor very differently across office, service, agriculture, production, operator, and elementary work.

Explaining Exposure

White-collar employment share is a strong structural predictor of national AI exposure. The paper also finds that log gross national income predicts exposure, but less strongly than workforce composition.

Predicting AI Adoption

National AI exposure predicts adoption statistics from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Microsoft, even though the three organizations publish different types of country-level usage measures.

Indirect Exposure Through Remittances

The remittance channel raises measured exposure among highly remittance-dependent countries: 18 of the 19 countries with remittances above 10% of GDP move above the diagonal in the paper's remittance analysis.
